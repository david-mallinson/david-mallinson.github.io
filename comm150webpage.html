Summary

Spotify's new policy change, set to remove royalties from a majority of its music catalog, has sparked significant backlash. The updated rules require songs to achieve about 200 annual streams before artists can earn royalties. If this threshold isn't met, the money that would have gone to the artist will instead be redirected to Spotify's general revenue pool. This change disproportionately affects smaller, independent artists, for whom reaching 200 streams can be a challenging goal. This policy is seen by some as a reverse Robin Hood scenario, taking from smaller artists to benefit larger ones. Despite Spotify's claim that the impact of this change will be negligible, it has been widely criticized within the industry. Additionally, Spotify plans to implement other changes, such as imposing fines on distributors involved in streaming fraud and adjusting royalty rules for "noise tracks" to combat exploitation of the system.
